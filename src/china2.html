Upside Down House in China


This house, as its name indicates, is located upside down, and was built by the Chinese authorities with the aim of encouraging tourism in the country.

In the Chinese region of Pantipeg, tourists can visit this house and enter it through the windows, specifically from the upper floor window, and the tour begins inside it in reverse.



Kingdom of the Little People


This kingdom, also known as the Kingdom of the Dwarves, is located in China, and it includes a group of residents whose height does not exceed 120 centimetres.

This village was established by Chinese businessman Chan Minjin, where he employed 100 people of short stature, and allocated them a separate kingdom that included its own police station, along with a fire station.

In addition, each of its workers receives a sum of money amounting to about $400 per month, in exchange for performing two shows a day in front of tourists.



Window to the World–Shenzhen


Visitors can go to China to visit one of its strangest tourist attractions, which is a park that covers an area of more than 480,000 square metres.

During the month of October, this campaign includes 130 copies highlighting the most prominent international tourist attractions, such as the Eiffel Tower and the Arc de Triomphe in France, in addition to the Pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx in Egypt, the Taj Mahal in India, the Tower of Pisa in Italy, and other historical and cultural monuments for each country.



Sheraton Huzhou Hotel


It is a newly built hotel, opened in 2013, and is located in the Chinese city of Huzhou.

This hotel is distinguished by its wonderful and exotic design that attracts rich tourists, due to its gentle horseshoe shape.
